Explanation

A body fat percentage estimate tells you what portion of your total body weight comes from fat mass. Unlike weight alone, body fat percentage can help explain changes in shape and fitness—especially if you are building muscle while losing fat.

This calculator uses common approaches such as the US Navy body fat method (based on body measurements like waist, neck, and height) and other widely used estimation formulas. These methods are convenient because they’re fast, affordable, and repeatable at home.

For best results, measure consistently: take measurements at the same time of day, keep the tape snug but not tight, and avoid measuring right after heavy meals or intense workouts. Small measurement differences can change the estimate—so focus on the trend over time.

If you need high precision (for clinical use), scans such as DEXA or professional assessments may be more accurate. For everyday health and fitness, a consistent method and regular tracking is usually more useful than a single “perfect” number.

Formula

Common methods include: US Navy (tape measurements), skinfold calipers, and bioelectrical impedance (BIA) estimates.

FAQs

Is a body fat percentage calculator exact?

No—most at-home methods are estimates. DEXA and other scans are typically more accurate, but measurement-based calculators are useful for tracking trends.

Is it good for tracking progress?

Yes. Use the same method and measure under similar conditions each time to track changes reliably.

Why do different formulas give different results?

Each formula is built from different datasets and assumptions. Population, body type, and measurement technique can all affect results.

Do athletes have different healthy ranges?

Often yes. Depending on sport and training, athletes may be healthy at lower body fat percentages than the general population.

Can children use this calculator?

For children and teens, use age-appropriate growth charts and guidance from a healthcare professional.
