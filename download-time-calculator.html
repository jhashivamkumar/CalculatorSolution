FAQs

Mb vs MB?

Mb = megabit, MB = megabyte. 8 bits = 1 byte. ISPs usually advertise Mbps, while files are shown in MB/GB.

Network overhead?

Protocol overhead and Wi-Fi conditions reduce effective throughput, so real downloads are often slower than the theoretical calculation.

Variable speed?

If your speed fluctuates, use the average speed you usually see in downloads for the best estimate.

Resumable downloads?

Resume typically continues from where it stopped and does not re-download completed parts, but behavior depends on the server/app.

Parallel downloads?

Parallel streams can improve throughput, but you may still be limited by router/ISP/server caps.
