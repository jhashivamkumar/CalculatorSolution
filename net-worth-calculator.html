Explanation

Your net worth is a snapshot of your financial position: assets (what you own) minus liabilities (what you owe). Tracking net worth over time helps you understand whether your saving, investing, and debt reduction strategies are working.

Tip: Update this quarterly. Use the same valuation method each time for consistent tracking.

Formula

Net Worth = Sum(Assets) - Sum(Liabilities)

FAQs

Include retirement accounts?

Yes. Include retirement accounts as assets, but remember withdrawal taxes/penalties may apply.

Market value?

Use current market value for investments and a reasonable estimate for property/vehicles.

Liabilities?

Include mortgages, loans, credit card balances, and any outstanding debt.

Frequency?

Monthly or quarterly updates are common to track progress and trends.

Goal setting?

Set targets based on your plans: debt-free goal, emergency fund, retirement, or major purchases.
