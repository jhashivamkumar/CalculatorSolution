Explanation

Your daily calorie needs are commonly estimated using two steps: BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) and TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure). BMR estimates calories your body burns at rest. TDEE multiplies BMR by an activity factor to reflect your lifestyle and exercise.

For goals: a small deficit (10‚Äì20%) is often used for weight loss, and a small surplus (10‚Äì15%) for lean gain. Macros here are optional estimates: protein based on body weight, fat as a % of calories, and remaining calories as carbs.
