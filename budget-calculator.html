Explanation

A budget is a simple plan for your money: how much comes in (income), how much goes out (expenses), and how much you keep (savings). This Budget Calculator helps you quickly estimate your monthly leftover and check whether your plan supports a savings goal. It also shows a year summary so you can see the long-term impact of small monthly changes.

A popular budgeting approach is the 50/30/20 rule: allocate about 50% to needs (rent, bills, groceries), 30% to wants (lifestyle), and 20% to savings and debt. This is not a strict ruleâ€”if rent is high, your "needs" may be higher. Use it as a guideline and adjust.

If your expenses exceed income, focus on the biggest levers first: reduce "wants", renegotiate recurring bills, and create a realistic debt plan. If you have a savings goal, automate it right after salary day so it becomes consistent.

FAQs

Start small?

Track expenses for a month to create a realistic budget. Then set limits category-wise.

Emergency fund?

Many people target 3â€“6 months of essential expenses as an emergency fund.

Debt payments?

Prioritize high-interest debt. Keep a small buffer, then pay more than the minimum when possible.

Automate savings?

Automate transfers on payday so savings happens before discretionary spending.

Adjust monthly?

Review monthly or whenever income/expenses change. Budgeting is a living plan.
