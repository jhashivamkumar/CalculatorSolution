Currency Converter – Explanation

A currency converter helps you calculate how much money you’ll receive when exchanging one currency for another. For example, converting USD to INR for international payments, or INR to EUR for travel budgeting. This calculator uses the latest published exchange rates and performs the conversion instantly in your browser.

Exchange rates change based on market demand, central bank policies, interest rates, inflation, and global events. Banks and payment providers may apply a spread (margin) and additional fees, so their final rate can differ from the mid-market rate shown by public sources.

Use this tool to compare scenarios quickly: test different amounts, swap currencies, or refresh to see the latest rate. For important transactions, confirm the exact rate and fees with your provider.

Currency Converter FAQs

Where do exchange rates come from in this currency converter?

This page fetches rates from a free currency API (Frankfurter). It returns the latest available rates and date, then we calculate the converted value in your browser.

Are currency conversion rates real-time?

Most free services provide the latest published rates (often updated on working days). For trading-level real-time pricing, you’d need paid market data feeds.

Why does my bank show a different amount?

Banks/fintech apps often add a spread and fees. This converter shows a reference rate, so the final charged amount can differ slightly.

Can I use this converter offline?

Live conversion needs the internet. Offline, you’d need to manually enter a rate and multiply it by your amount.
