How EMI Works

A concise guide to understanding equated monthly instalments (EMI), what affects them, and practical tips to manage loan repayments.

What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment — the fixed payment amount a borrower pays to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. The EMI consists of both principal and interest components.

Formula & Example

The standard EMI formula is:

EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1)

Where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100), and n = total number of monthly installments.

Practical tips

  • Shorter tenures reduce total interest but increase monthly payments.
  • Small differences in interest rate can change total cost significantly over long tenures.
