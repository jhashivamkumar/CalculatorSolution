Explanation

The Area Calculator helps you find the area of common geometric shapes such as a circle, rectangle, square, and triangle. Area means the amount of surface covered inside a boundary, and it is always expressed in square units (for example: m², cm², ft², in²).

This calculator is useful in everyday situations like estimating flooring area, carpet size, paint coverage for walls, tile calculations, and even land or plot measurements. Students can use it to verify geometry homework quickly, while professionals can use it to get fast estimates before detailed measurement.

To get accurate results, keep units consistent. For example, if you enter length in meters (m), also enter width in meters (m), and the result will be in square meters (m²). For circles, be careful to use radius (half of diameter) when applying πr². If you only know the diameter, convert it first: radius = diameter ÷ 2.

The formulas shown below are standard geometry formulas. This page runs entirely in your browser, so calculations are fast and private. For critical work (architecture/engineering/legal land measurement), always double-check with professional tools or official measurements.

Formula

Circle Area:     A = π × r²
Rectangle Area:  A = length × width
Square Area:     A = side²
Triangle Area:   A = ½ × base × height

FAQs

What units should I use for area?

Area is measured in square units like m², cm², ft² or in². Use the same unit for all inputs to avoid mistakes.

How do I calculate area for irregular shapes?

Break the shape into smaller known shapes (rectangles, triangles, circles), calculate each area, then add them together.

Circle: should I enter radius or diameter?

The standard formula uses radius (r). If you only have diameter (d), convert first: r = d/2.

Why does my answer look too big or too small?

This often happens due to mixed units (cm with m) or using diameter instead of radius. Convert units and verify the correct input.

Where is area used in real life?

Area is used for flooring, painting, tiles, land/plot calculations, agriculture planning, and geometry education.
